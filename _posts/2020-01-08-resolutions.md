Greetings fellow denizens of this celestial rock ball to which we are tethered!

We have done it. We have passed through 'the year which shall not be named' (and let's not forget 2019 either!), stepped into a new decade, and taken our first breaths of that 2021 air! We all experienced many hardships this past year physically, mentally, occupationally, financially, spiritually, and more. Through these hardships, we have seen the ebb and flow of our collective incivilities, overwhelmingly heartfelt displays of humanity, devastating and long-foretold tolls being extracted from our ecosystems, and a global spirit of activism higher than it has been at any time I can remember. Hell, as I am writing this we are in the middle of one of the most [laughably amazing events class warfare](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/events/gamestop-stock-surge) in modern history and I know this is just the start of the 2021 rollercoaster about as much as I know Epstein didn't kill himself!

While so much change is motion, there wouldn't be a better time than now to go ahead and clear out a whole host of other problems before we settle back into the momentous swing of social _normality_. I don't see us going all the way on that though, so the next best thing we all can do is help out Dr. King by improving the content of our characters!

![Yay! Boooo!](/assets/post-content/resolutions/yay-boo.jpg){:width="400"}


I'm not typically one for new year's resolutions but given the year we all just had I think we could all use it so why not! Having now approached the second month of the year, I've thought about the general direction I'd like to take life, goals, plans, things I'd like to improve, etc. Nothing is concrete but I think it will give me a solid bit of stuff to write about throughout the year.


## 2021 Personal To-do list, GO!
- I think it's safe to say that last year I was not my best, physically or mentally. Since one of the best ways to improve the mind is to improve the body, it's on the list. I am also of the belief you cannot truly have a new years resolution list without including exercise! Perhaps the collective reinforcement of attempted exercise resolutions will one day pay off!
- I'd like to work on tracking time better and just fixing some of my terrible time management skills.
- Some short time set aside daily to reintegrate a meditative practice to help keep a clearer head and stay better focused on short-term goals.
- I was also thinking of looking into a cheap, FitBit-style smart wristband to collect some biometric data for this quantified-self experiment in a passive way. My main target for this would be to help track health and body rhythms at night to try and improve my sleep! Whether I go that route or not, I am undecided. I will report more on my decision later.
- I have a few unfinished web projects lying around that I would like to get completed and two outdated Android apps that need to be 'refreshed' to a modern Android codebase. I have not dipped my toes into the Android pool for a bit so it will be interesting to see the changes to the ecosystem.
- Work on some Python mini-projects to broaden my experience in data science, data viz, and AI.
- I rebuild my home network recently and will probably do a little blog series on it.
- There are a few Google educator certificates that aren't too difficult to obtain!
- A few renewed technical certs would be nice as well.
- Put in some solid practice hours on the guitar!


I suppose a decent way of adhering to resolutions is through public accountability, so let's try it out for science! I hope that some of the positive collective humanity expressed last year will show itself in spades on this revolution around our favorite fireball! I know we have the capacity for greatness when we put ourselves into it. My wish for this year is that we can make a meaningful, positive, and lasting impact on our collective society, the life of another, and our world as a whole, despite the looming challenges at our door!

What is on your list for the year, friend? What is your wish for the times ahead?

Until next time,
David
